I'm Youssef Amir, but you can call me Amir. I am a programmer, writer, debater, presenter, and researcher. I'm currently enrolled in an Honors curriculum with a STEM emphasis at STEM High School for Boys – 6th of October. To reach out to uncharted knowledge spheres and be supported in my quest to study and explore new areas, I want to pursue higher education in a college that value and improve its students.

My desire in life is to leave a mark behind me wherever I go and to make a change on my community, my country, and the whole world and I try to fulfill this desire in everything I participate in. In the following sections, I'll take you on a journey through this world of mine. So, come along for this ride and get to know me better.

What I do?

Programming

Although I only started learning programming in 2021, I couldn’t help but find myself passionate about it. Since technology is now a main part of everyone’s life, I was fascinated with how working on different useful projects can benefit others. Moreover, I was enthusiastic about coding and problem-solving. After a while, I began a new journey in Competitive Programming. I enjoyed problem-solving and I took this as a chance for me to enhance my programming skills and enrich my knowledge of data structure and algorithms, which will be extremely helpful when working on big projects in the future.

Writing

Since my early childhood, I have been passionate about writing. I found it fascinating to express my thoughts and feelings in words. In high school, I started growing this passion, joining IEEE as a Magazine writer to journal events in the fields of Computer Science and Engineering. After that, I founded my school’s first magazine. We developed a first issue, and we’re currently working on a website that will feature weekly articles about new school events and accomplishments. Furthermore, I have been committed to write for 30 minutes daily for the last 3 years, and some of my articles can be found in the blog below.

Debating

My interest in debating raised massively with my participation in STEM MUN 21’ Security Council Conference. As a delegate, I debated around Afghanistan war, and was honored the best delegate. Since then, I have participated in many conferences, and I loved how I devoted my research, reasoning, and public speaking skills. Later, I became the president of STEM MUN, chairing its 22’ conference. Moreover, as the head of the scientific committee at my School Student Union, I utilized this skill to present students’ opinions and problems.

Courses

What I learned

  • 2020
    Writing in The Sciences
    Stanford University

    This course helped improve my writing skills, especially in research. The course tackled principles of effective writing, choice of strong verbs, and writing organization. Furthermore, it discussed scientific manuscript, peer review process, ethics of scientific publication, plagiarism, how to write a review paper.

  • 2021
    CS50
    Harvard University

    This course teaches students how to think algorithmically and solve problems efficiently. Topics include abstraction, algorithms, data structures, encapsulation, resource management, security, software engineering, and web programming. Languages include C, Python, and SQL plus HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Problem sets inspired by the arts, humanities, social sciences, and sciences. Course culminates in a final project.

  • 2022
    Competitive Programming: Bronze and Silver Sections
    USACO Guide

    I deepened my understanding of competitive programming, learned various algorithms, and worked on many problems from previous USACO and IOI contests. The two sections' curriculum tackled Complete Search, Sorting & Sets, Greedy Algorithm, Prefix Sums, Sorting & Searching, and basics of Graph Theory.

  • 2021
    Leading Diverse Teams & Organizations
    University of Michigan

    In the past two years, I had experiences in many leadership positions such as being the President of STEM MUN, Founder and President of School Magazine, and Vice President of STEM Mentorship. Hence, I studied this course to enhance my leadership skills and create engaging, diverse, equitable, and positive environment.

  • 2021
    Crash Course On Python
    Google

    I understood the basics of Python and wrote simple programs with it. I learned how the building blocks of programming fit together and combined all of this knowledge to solve complex programming problems. I got hands-on experience with programming concepts through interactive exercises and real-world examples.
